Agentforce

Routine work moves to the agent. Staff handle the exceptions.

A coordinator fielding enrollment questions by email and phone. A scheduler manually reconciling rooms and instructors in a spreadsheet. A security officer collecting clearance paperwork before a badge request can even begin. Agentforce makes this the old way. Every agent action is also an invocable Apex method, so it works as a Flow step if a GovCloud+ AI feature is not yet FedRAMP High authorized at deployment time.

Phase 1 · Enrollment
Enrollment Concierge
Answers enrollment questions, checks eligibility and prerequisites, guides trainees and agency nominators through the portal, and surfaces waitlist status without staff involvement.
A course cycle generates hundreds of standard inquiries about eligibility, prerequisites, seat availability, and waitlist position. Every one of those is answered instantly, 24/7, through the portal. Coordinators handle policy exceptions and billing disputes. Not inbox triage.
Phase 2 · Scheduling
Scheduling Assistant
Finds available rooms and instructors, resolves scheduling conflicts, books group events, and notifies affected parties when changes occur.
Finding an available room, cross-referencing instructor calendars, confirming the booking, and notifying attendees is a 25-minute coordination task per booking. The agent does it in under two minutes. Schedulers spend their time on facilities planning, not calendar reconciliation.
Phase 4 · Badging
Badging Coordinator
Processes badge requests, verifies clearance reference data, routes requests through the approval workflow, and coordinates with the access control system for provisioning.
The agent validates clearance reference data, populates the badge request record, and routes it to security staff with everything already filled in. Security reviews and approves. They stop being data collectors and start being decision makers.
